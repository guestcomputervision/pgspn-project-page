PGSPN: Physics-Guided Spatial Propagation Network
for Sparsity-Robust Underwater Depth Completion

Abstract

Depth completion has achieved remarkable progress in terrestrial environments. However, these environments predominantly assume uniformly distributed sparse depth across scenes. Unlike terrestrial scenarios, underwater environments present two critical challenges. First, sparse depth typically exhibits highly irregular or spatially biased distributions. Second, extensive regions without depth measurements, such as open water, exacerbate depth bleeding, erroneous propagation of depth values from supervised into unsupervised areas. To address these challenges, we propose PGSPN, to our knowledge, the first physics-guided underwater depth completion framework. Rather than relying solely on irregular sparse measurements, PGSPN leverages underwater optical physics to construct a sparsity-robust metric prior. Specifically, we estimate per-channel transmission maps from the input image and rescale them to metric scale, generating a physics-based prior that remains reliable even under extreme sparsity. Then, to prevent depth bleeding during refinement, we introduce attenuation-aware spatial propagation that reduces propagation weights for pixels with large depth discrepancies. Finally, experiments on FLSea-Stereo and FLSea-VI demonstrate state-of-the-art performance with superior robustness in handling irregular sparsity patterns.
